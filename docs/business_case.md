# Business Case

## Problem
Retail and healthcare distributors need enough inventory to meet demand without over-investing in excess stock. Poor forecasts drive stockouts, excess working capital, waste, and poor replenishment decisions.

## Users
- Demand planners
- Inventory analysts
- Supply chain managers
- Commercial analytics teams

## Decisions Supported
- Which SKUs need replenishment attention
- How much to reorder
- Where stockout risk is concentrated
- How service level or lead time changes affect recommendations

## KPIs
- Forecast error
- Forecast bias
- Service level proxy
- Stockout risk rate
- Days of cover
- Reorder quantity

## Value To Supply Chain
The project demonstrates how forecast outputs can be translated into operational decisions rather than stopping at model metrics.

## McKesson / Pharma Relevance
The same logic can support pharmaceutical distribution workflows such as service-level planning, inventory prioritization, stockout monitoring, and days-of-supply analysis.

## Limitations
- Uses public retail data rather than healthcare distribution data
- Does not model expiry, cold-chain constraints, or supplier contracts
- Acts as an applied MVP rather than a fully productionized enterprise platform
